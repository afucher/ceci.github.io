Tools for Change

Leaders have good reasons to ask the people in their organizations to change: a competitor launches a new product, material prices rise, a new technology changes the way you do business. Maybe you’ve tried to implement such a change only to find that people hang onto their old ways. And maybe you’ve asked yourself, "Why is it that people resist my well-thought out and reasonable requests to change?"

Data: 01/10/2018 Horário: 10h às 18h Local: Campinas, SP Inscreva-se para Tools for Change

Ementa

  1. Introductions
  2. Experiences with change
    • High and low point past experiences
    • We we can learn about organizational change and influence from these experiences.
  3. Environmental Scan
    • 3.1. Helping the people in the system see their system
    • 3.2.Putting the past, present, and future in perspective
  4. Force Field
    • Identifying what contributes to the present pattern, that doesn’t serve the future
    • Identify points of action
  5. Shape of the change
    • Identifying ripple effects
    • Identifying potential people impacts, both positive and negative
  6. Designing Experiments
    • Considerations
    • Observations
  7. Creating boundary stories
    • Understanding the need for local variation and adaptation
    • Using stories to help bound appropriate variation
  8. Wrap-up

Coaching Teams

An experiential workshop where participants will learn to observe and assess enabling conditions for teamwork, team interactions, and dynamics. Participants will gain knowledge need to improve conditions that are hampering teams. Through simulations and small group practice, participants will hone their abilities to analyze group dynamics, and take effective action.

Data: 02/10/2018 Horário: 10h às 18h Local: Campinas, SP Inscreva-se para Coaching Teams

Ementa

  1. Introductions
  2. Experiences with teams
    • High and low point teams in past experience
    • simulated team experience
  3. Foundations for collaboration and teamwork
  4. Understanding sources of motivation and demotivation
    • Hackman ends/means matrix
    • Autonomy
  5. Observing team dynamics
    • What to observe
    • Practice
    • Sharing observations
  6. Choosing to intervene
    • Making sense of your observation
    • Adapting the environment
  7. Diagnosing conflict
    • Sources of conflict
    • Strategies for navigating conflict successfully
  8. Wrap-up
